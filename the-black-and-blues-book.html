It was said even Charlie, Dan, and the rest of them fell prey to the black and blues So and so had gone up north,
and no one could get a hold of grass, though there was coke in every public bathroom from Glasgow to Shetland.
No one had the time for even coffee or for a half of whatever, any longer.
A certain thickening of the blood and of the air was observed as light bent out of shape, it did not seem to move as it was once want to, some years ago.
That who-did-you-know got concrete feet, and when I wished upon a teevee satellite some evening, a quarter to witching hour, I was answered the following morning by a jovial JML presenter, who, propped upon my television stand, announced candidly it might never happen, but when it did, it would happen sort of sideways, and I wouldn't know the difference anyway.

There were whisperings and malingerings around known locations, much like whispers while the walls crawl. And when they quit the crawling, they slowed to a stop, rolled into the by-lane and the engine cut, then nothing but red glare of hazards in the middle of the night.

There were no places called home any longer, for anyone, and we all sort of slid by one another and tipped our caps. And goddamn you if you didn't know the handshake because we know now, you were never one of us. Our legs got stripped of all fat and nothing but sinew, nothing but muscle on us, and a thin coat for the winter, because we were all on our minimum wage feet, all the time. And you could say nothing to us which could offend.
And you could do nothing to us that would hurt.
And there was very little to account for in the bank.
And sometimes you would turn around and look to find another week or month had passed by too quick to see, you'd been living so fast, so fast you were living a second at a time, splitting moments into their constituent parts like atoms.

Well,
it was around the time all this was happening that some guy figured he'd talk about himself for a minute. To say we were friends would say too much or too little, but we dug each-other's minds in the strange way you sometimes happen to do. We'd known each-other by face - though not name - when, one chill November afternoon, I found him passed out in a bar stool with a notebook in his
hand. My attempts to make meaningful contact went unnoticed, as he was frightfully pissed. We hadn't seen each-other in some years though, and I was interested in where he'd been; the murder squads had wiped out most of the undesirables and the creatives some years ago. Not that I need to remind you, but we inhabit more enlightened times now, don't we?

In any case, with the possibility for conversation minimal, I took the notebook from his hands and read what he had to say. While I didn't find much in particular you might call likeable, it resonated with me the way certain sounds do, like a far-off train. I was still sitting next to him, reading, when he awoke some hours later, the bar festooned with empty schooners, and myself rather the worse for wear. After he had hurriedly reclaimed the standard-margin, he and I traded battle scars and raps, and we continued to meet on irregular occasions throughout the remaining years of his life. The conversations we had, though I never truly appreciated them at the time, rank amongst the most intriguing I've enjoyed in the quiet years since my retreat from the scene.

Despite the candour we enjoyed in these meetings, one subject we never discussed was the notebook I'd read that day in the bar. I suppose much of it expounded on experiences too particular to him to be of much interest to anyone else. Despite the literary ambitions of his younger, less inebriated years, I think that by the time of writing, writing itself had become for him more of an evacuation, and an emptiness, than a means of communication. That said, there was always something in him
which seemed to spite this, a voice that wanted thrown in with other people's, and this trace element, I expect, is what resonated within me all those years ago. A sort of ghost in the shell.

The manuscript to which I refer, and which I here preface, bore more resemblance to an extended face-book post than a work of literary significance (or otherwise). Regardless, I couldn't help but find a certain quality in what he said, haggard and dissolute though it may be. I suppose that says more about myself and he, the times we lived through, than it does about the work itself. But now
that he's gone wherever people go, and has taken the tonsure one last time, before one last walk down that narrow road, I thought I might post his narcissistic rag and see if anyone felt the same as I did - as I still do - about what he wrote.

Anyway, here he is, in his own words; singing drunk in the shower, tripping over skips in the vinyl, whilst, high, the world rolls by, or even, yes, perhaps even stops for him - now and again.

I don't know where I'll sleep tonight. it's october and I haven't washed in a week, and september or yesterday seems dead and gone and about nearly forgotten. I remember I was supposed to be writing something, but I left all my shit in the wrong place, someplace I can't go back to. thoughts half formed and thrown up on cocktail napkins, supermarket receipts, the backs of hands. perhaps I may fix to go back there and collect them. "perhaps" is fast becoming the salient term in my vocabulary. it is the fugue in my process of indecision, which weighs the options in each hand, somehow forgets what it's doing before a choice is made, and instead shits in the kitchen sink. I need somewhere to sleep tonight, but I don't know where. hot water and a hand-job, or some other superfluous grace note of hospitality to distract me from futile humanism. there are cars slashed burning on the black. the nights are drawing in, so gather your wits up with your skirts and hope. not for something fucking stupid, like world peace or equality; hope for a clean pillow slip, a lamp-shade, slippers, for the sound sugar makes as it sinks into tea. societies aren't built with ideals, but with bricks and glucose.

no men on the moon, then, no tomorrows. only singular exhalations of smoke into the ether, water vapour cast up by the shower, coiling in a sun-beam. singular frames, atoms of material reality. inhabiting these images, remaining attentive to the fact that you are always closer to now than to later, because "now" is something you can touch. you can throw "now" down onto a bed and acquaint yourself with it. "later" and "before" are twins that promise sacred displays of tribadism and deliver only glances and excuses.

I walk out into late afternoon cigarette smoke, hot sugar smell, far off screaming. the unmistakable report of gun-fire. blink in the light after two hours in the dark. some movie. guns and androids and the end of days as we know it. I might remark upon the inventive use of cinematography in the closing scenes to imply an end to our noxious marriage to technology, and a restitution of our relationship with nature. if waxing poetic, I would add the intra-android caste system paralleled neatly with contemporary social inequality, as man-made as the synthetic alloy and circuit-boards therein. however, I don't wish to insult your intelligence, and I don't really buy any of that bullshit. suffice it to say, the spectacle sated certain vicarious appetites, and allowed more pressing appetites to go numb in the dark.

I might not buy it, the bullshit, but that doesn't mean it's not somewhat enjoyable to talk it. we all walk through the same gallery, art music and films - it can be interesting what people attend to and what they make of it. though, even when I'm most immersed in it, I can't forget the cost of the entry

writers are supposed to say things universally agreed upon, but agreed upon silently. they sort of spoil the joke of life to seem smart at parties. I've never felt terribly smart at parties. and I guess that I've never felt much like a writer. furthermore, in spite of this sacred duty to common denominators, I've always felt unable to marry myself to these things we are meant, as world citizens, to deem important. I suppose it's kind of funny how that feeling should be felt by so many. they might read something, like this perhaps, and feel not quite so alone or helpless, they might pass me, alone and helpless, on the street. neither of us would know, and we'd still feel alone and helpless, separately. that's the cold fact; that we hold up our ideas of how we might feel to escape how we do feel, and always will.

like, for instance, now, when all that is rational in me knows that this, whatever it was, has ended, and I'll return to beige normalcy and forget the piquancy of each moment with her. they'll be smoothed over and rewritten by memory. and even now, knowing this, doesn't change a thing. I am wilfully deceiving myself.
I guess what I'm trying to say is that writers are liars.
whether they drink too much or fuck around, well, those things are questions of aesthetics. anytime someone thinks an empty epigram is important enough to be written down - they're lying. and furthermore, they're only writing because they're alone and there's nothing to wrap their dick around. pardon my being course, but it is what it is.
why do you think I'm writing this down now, rather than saying these things to her instead?

we duck into a close briefly, to avoid a patrol. it's beginning to get dark. I hand over her papers. she'll need them later.

supposedly a writer should be honest. but some of the worst things you've ever heard were the most honest, and recited in the most objective of tones. planes crash. kittens die. why would you want to make a career breaking bad news to people, unless you sort of enjoyed ruining people's days? I suppose if your day's been ruined already, it's the next best thing to a good one - for both of us, you and I.
what does it matter to anyone but me that I've walked through fire? should any of us have? and don't we always want the quiet life as surely as the cool girl from school, whose thrall we never truly escape from. and I am so high and drunk and removed, and now it all means nothing as a consequence. and never will. I don't have one spare fuck. I shall retain it, and save it for something worthwhile. all the books and the sidewalks burn. I loved you and you didn't, besides what I thought, and now I look, as for a standard-margin in a labryinthe library, half forgotten, for the mystery and the minotaur.
ah fuck it. shoot the craw with the dog in the backyard. I must once again be the moving man.

I can only hope that my sufferings do not become so particular I can no longer speak of them with other people. suffering is only particular to the suffering, however. it's a sort of law which binds us all together, and the anticipation of it, and preparation for its diminution, is a shared responsibility. we have all been on the bottom. we all know what it is to feel the whip. we are none of us the same,
but we are all of us equally susceptible to circumstance, to contingency, to the whip. if all of us can be beaten... we could try to be more careful, and more caring of one another. I'm thinking of these things without speaking as she walks away and I follow behind her. if I am uncaring, I am at least a careful student of ironies.

mistakes like these are a young man's luxury. I look at old men and the way they sit in chairs and keep their voices to themselves, without moving. for a while I didn't understand their indolence, and now I know that whenever a man moves, or fixes to make one, trouble is on its way. trouble often carries something sharp, long, and heavy, and is not acquainted with prophylactics. despite this, I want to make a move, or say something, even if it's a second hand armchair idea that no one much cares for.

but sometimes the only thing we can do for someone is say goodbye as they march into the meat grinder. I don't feel it right now, but I'd like to believe that looking at this, as it happens, this terrible thing, is a preparation for saving the next one. but I don't know for sure, and I don't know for sure that writing these things down does she or I or whoever might come next, much good at all. I'm free climbing the mystery with my eyes closed, no rope but threadbare, tenuous semantics, some cheap wordplay, a modicum of literary education. but most all of the black and blues, to show and tell.

it's got something to do with life and much to do with death when we realise the things we love most are people, and people are capable of buying a ticket and getting on a bus/plane/zeppelin, and leaving us forever. but it is often the looking, as they walk away, and the thinking as we try and draw them back to us through thought alone, (the thinking on what we might say, write, or perform to them) that is the making of us. our final consolation and reconciliation with the fact of our life is that it will end.

what is a writer or a poet if not someone who publicises their revelations, however modest they might be? and so what makes them any better than those whose revelations remain private? how do you place value on epiphany? and isn't epiphany cheapened by translation? isn't that moment when we look at what we love deepened, when it looks back and asks us, "aye whit," and we reply, "nothing," and smile? and if not, then what? I ask myself this question as I attempt to communicate the feeling, knowing it is in no language but in the tongue as it bends around another’s. and isn't that, now, the only language I want to learn or practice? what do you want me to say, when I don't really want to say anything - but when saying things seems to be the only thing I can do?

we're at the station.
there's time for a cigarette, and the anxious search for the perfect goodbye words. I miraculously catch the scent of green, I see a schoolgirl put some trash in the bin instead of ditch it on the pavement, and I am reminded of the insuperable nature of betterment, which moves slowly, aye, but moves surely. and my amazement is in how little of it has to do with what people read in books. books tend to complicate things which should remain simple. still she and I say nothing.

I prepare for the hour when the mind does not answer, and toughen up my leather. I am the man that scoops up the tired and the weary where they lie. I have a fashionable pair of shoes and a bottle of micellar cleansing solution. I think of buildings burning and the calm objectivity of rifles, while she is climbing the stairs onto the bus. her right lace is untied. she waves and I would like to mention it to her, but sometimes silence is better. I think I will die of embarrassment if the last thing I say to her is that she can't tie her own shoes, so I just wave back.

it would be reassuringly appropriate to say I will hop on the next greyhound to nowhere, or even move a few feet to my left. instead I will stand where I am and most likely will remain for some time, smiling, and thinking of noose arms around necks. the bus is gone. I don't know where it's going.

in the meantime, it is october, I still don't have a place to sleep, and the world is still ending. I stand and smoke, and think a while. I'm still supposed to be writing that thing, you know. perhaps I will.
